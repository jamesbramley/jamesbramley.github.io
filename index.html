  • Tower Survival Game

    Play in Browser

    DestructibleEnvironment.cs
    MenuSystem.cs
    TowerManager.cs

    I came up with the idea for this game when I remembered some of the levels I used to play on 'LittleBigPlanet' for the Playstation 3. However, I wanted to add more depth and so in this game I included a variety of gameplay elements like powerups and a slow-down time mechanic.

    The game features multiple playable characters each with different stats that affect the way they play. To add some objective to the game, high scores are recorded and many things are locked at first. The player must collect coins while they play with which they can purchase the extra content like new characters, music tracks and environments.

    All of the character, bomb and UI assets were created by me. The game started as a collaborative project, but I have since gone solo. As such, some code was not done by me; the camera movement and four of the bomb types were not coded by me, but everything else was. The music heard in the video is placeholder music - 'Another Winter' by Anamanaguchi from Scott Pilgrim vs The World (PS3).

  • Racing Game Prototype

    CameraMovement.cs
    RoadSegment.cs

    I wanted to make a 3D racing game prototype as I am a fan of arcade racers and wanted to try my hand at a genre of game I hadn't yet worked on. Within Unity, there isn't a simple way to create and modify roads, so I implemented some procedural mesh generation to make the racetrack easier to construct based on this Unite 2015 talk. I also created a camera movement system which adapts its position based on the speed and position of the car, which helps give the game a sense of speed absent from a more static camera. Car model taken from the Unity asset store.

  • Virtual Reality

    PlayerMovement.cs
    Controller.cs
    Task.cs
    InteractableObject.cs

    For my 3rd year project at university, I decided to produce a VR experience. In addition to producing an immersive experience, the aim of this project was to investigate different methods of interaction, as well as the different activities/challenges that could be done in VR, in order to see how they affect a user's immersion levels. This includes the effect things like nausea can have on a player's immersion within VR and the ways they can be alleviated.

  • Football Simulation

    Match.cs
    PitchPlayer.cs
    FixtureGenerator.cs

    During the 2020 lockdown and the absence of football, I found myself wanting to create a football simulation game that was a happy medium between the involved nature of Football Manager and the less in-depth FIFA.

    I created a system that simulates games based on the stats of different players. In a match, the football pitch is split into a 7x3 grid, within which players can move. Players make decisions and perform actions based on their stats, the position of the ball, their team's current formation, and the positions of their teammates. Better players make better decisions and execute actions more successfully.

    To save inputting data for 200 teams and ~5000 players, I generate them from databases of city names, countries, first names, and surnames. I gave all of the players randomly assigned values for each of their attributes, based on an initial 'player quality' and using a Gaussian distribution.

  • Original Card Game

    Rulebook PDF

    CardLoader.cs

    DeckEditCard.cs
    Duel.cs

    I came up with the idea to create a card game based around the Gaming Society my friends and I attend at University. Initially I came up with the system for the game, then my friends and I created over 200 cards with different effects and stats based on the in-jokes of our Gaming society. We held a tournament for it at our 24 hour Gaming Society event. At the moment the functionality is that of a real life playing mat with a few additional features.
