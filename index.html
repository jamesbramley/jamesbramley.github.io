Personal Projects

  • 2D Platformer

    DOWNLOAD

    During my 4th year at university I took a game development submodule. In this submodule I was tasked with creating a game in which you collected 10 'treasures' around the Durham University campus. I created a 2D platformer entitled 'Durham Quest V: Final Treasure Prologue'. The game uses a traditional platformer design combined with a small hint of Metroidvania.

    The game was developed by me over the course of 2 months, is completely finished and features 4 levels. My aim with this game was to keep the project scale low but have a polished final product. As the game is quite short, I added a rank system that challenges players in order to add some replay value. To get the best rank for a level you must collect all of its treasure while also finishing in a quick time.

    The character design and UI elements were created by me, however other assets were taken from pre-existing sources to save time.

  • Original Card Game

    Rulebook PDF

    I came up with the idea to create a card game based around the Gaming Society my friends and I attend at University. Initially I came up with the system for the game, then my friends and I created over 200 cards with different effects and stats based on the in-jokes of our Gaming society. The aesthetic we went for was 'extremely crude MS paint'.

    After the cards were created, I made a virtual playing mat for the cards in which 2 players could build decks and face off against each other. We held a tournament for it at our 24 hour Gaming Society event. At the moment the functionality is that of a real life playing mat with a few additional features, however when I have time in the future I intend to extend it into a fully polished digital card game.

  • Virtual Reality

    For my 3rd year project at university, I decided to produce a VR experience. In addition to producing an immersive experience, the aim of this project was to investigate different methods of interaction, as well as the different activities/challenges that could be done in VR, in order to see how they affect a user's immersion levels. This includes the effect things like nausea can have on a player's immersion within VR and the ways they can be alleviated.

    The project was produced in Unity, using the SteamVR plugin.
